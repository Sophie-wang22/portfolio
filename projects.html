The Warm But Refracted Glow of Corporate Social Responsibility
Dartmouth Economics Department • Summer 2020
This project seeks to develop a framework in which social entrepreneurship can be evaluated as a means for individuals to achieve their philanthropic objectives, alongside existing opportunities like the public and non-profit sectors. We focus on how people express their different preferences in their multiple roles as consumers, workers, investors, and voters. This project considers how those preferences get aggregated, the actions that follow from that aggregation, and the impacts on individual and social welfare.
Development of a Novel Copepod-Based Ovitrap for Mosquito Control
CT Science Fair • Winter 2018
Mosquito-control systems that are inexpensive, environmentally-sound and low-maintenance are lacking. Cyclopoid copepods, small aquatic organisms that have the ability to destroy mosquito larvae, have been used in Vietnam and other places for mosquito control. Utilizing copepods’ larvae-destroying ability, this project developed a copepod-based ovitrap that is cheap, environmentally friendly and can reduce larvae population by 95% to serve as a mosquito-control solution for residential communities.
